## Discussion Notes

### 1. Business Case Review
All core sections now drafted. Team reviewed key outputs:

**Business Options (AA):** Four options analysed:
1. Maintain existing workflows — Rejected (AMI mortality continues, non-compliant with State Govt directive)
2. Advanced Dispatcher Training — Rejected (marginal improvement, doesn't scale)
3. Direct EMS without AI — Rejected (unacceptable patient safety risk without triage support)
4. AI-Supported EMS Dispatch System — **Approved** (addresses all clinical, operational and regulatory needs)

**Expected Benefits (AK + JO):** Five-column table structure adopted. Key benefits confirmed:
- 20–40% reduction in AMI mortality/morbidity (consistent with CRT clinical evidence)
- Reduction in average symptom-to-treatment time by 15–30%
- Reduction in unnecessary ambulance dispatches by 10–20%

**Costs (AA):** CAPEX finalised at $209,824.75. OPEX $80,436/year. Efficiency savings $1,100,280/year.

### 2. Investment Appraisal
LH presented draft appraisal:
- Year 1 surplus: $810,019.25 (after CAPEX + OPEX)
- Payback period: ~2.5 months
- ROI: ~269%
- Budget neutrality achieved within Year 1

Team agreed this is compelling and well-supported by the cost model. JO to do final proofread.

### 3. Major Risks (NM)
Five critical/high risks documented:
- AI false negatives (Critical) — mitigation: AI acts as decision support only; 80% minimum sensitivity gate
- NSW Health Privacy legislation non-compliance (Critical)
- Increased direct EMS call volume (High)
- Cultural resistance from dispatch/ambulance (Medium)
- Budget neutrality not achieved (High)
